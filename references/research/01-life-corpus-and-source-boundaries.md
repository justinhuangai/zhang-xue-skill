# Life, Corpus, and Source Boundaries

This file maps Zhang Xue's life path, operator arc, public corpus, and source boundaries through April 18, 2026.
It is not hero worship. It is the discipline layer that decides what belongs in a Zhang Xue skill and what should be left out.

## Chronology and role boundaries

### 2006 to 2008 — Special stunt rider and mechanic period
- Observation: ZXMOTO's official company introduction places Zhang Xue in the Chongqing New Feeling off-road team as both stunt rider and mechanic.
- Why it matters: This is the earliest stable signal that his public persona is not merely media-born enthusiasm; it starts from riding, wrenching, and performance constraints.
- Modern transfer: Treat this as the base of the skill's engineering legitimacy, not as a romantic origin myth.

### 2009 to 2012 — Product responsibility in the off-road factory context
- Observation: The official timeline says Zhang Xue worked at Apollo off-road in a product-responsible role.
- Why it matters: This matters because it shifts the story from individual rider identity toward system-level product accountability.
- Modern transfer: The skill should preserve the operator-builder transition rather than flattening him into a racer.

### 2013 to 2016 — Huanghe motorcycle R&D team
- Observation: ZXMOTO's official about page places Zhang Xue in Huanghe's R&D and design team and links him to concrete model outcomes.
- Why it matters: This is an important bridge between hands-on mechanical intuition and organized product development.
- Modern transfer: Use this period to justify why the repo cares about manufacturability, not just desire and willpower.

### 2017 — Co-founding KOVE
- Observation: Multiple public profiles and brand-side narratives agree that Zhang Xue became a co-founder of KOVE in 2017.
- Why it matters: This is the first large, durable organizational stage of his public business identity.
- Modern transfer: The skill should treat this as a key proving ground for product line expansion, team building, and rally/racing ambition.

### 2017 to 2024 — KOVE as the pre-ZXMOTO proving ground
- Observation: The official ZXMOTO bio lists several KOVE product families and specifically links Zhang Xue to heavy-hitting models in that period.
- Why it matters: Without this stage, the later ZXMOTO leap can be mistaken for sudden luck or pure personality force.
- Modern transfer: Preserve the continuity between earlier product programs and later founder bets.

### 2023 — Dakar rally relevance
- Observation: The official profile highlights leadership of the KOVE rally team at Dakar as a milestone involving Chinese brand, Chinese rider, and Chinese machine.
- Why it matters: This is an early example of the way Zhang Xue uses elite validation as industrial proof rather than merely marketing content.
- Modern transfer: The repo should keep validation logic visible before the WorldSSP breakthrough.

### 2024 March — Exit from KOVE
- Observation: Public timelines, including encyclopedia-style summaries and later profiles, mark Zhang Xue's departure from KOVE in March 2024.
- Why it matters: This is a real structural break, not a footnote. It separates inherited organizational context from the next fully self-owned brand bet.
- Modern transfer: The skill should recognize this as a reset point for product, team, and narrative control.

### 2024 April — Founding ZXMOTO / 张雪机车
- Observation: Both the brand's official material and public records align on 2024 as the founding year of the new company.
- Why it matters: This is where the repo's main object begins. Everything after this point must be read through founder judgment under sharper resource constraints.
- Modern transfer: Use this date as the core brand boundary for the skill.

### 2024 September — 500RR revealed publicly
- Observation: ZXMOTO's official global site records the 500RR launch around the 2024 CIMAMotor cycle and frames it as the brand's first model.
- Why it matters: It shows that the new brand chose product-first visibility rather than a vague brand-only debut.
- Modern transfer: Treat early product release as part of the proof chain, not merely brand theater.

### 2025 January to March — Track tests and first deliveries
- Observation: The official global site repeatedly foregrounds track tests, launch events, and first-batch deliveries for the 500RR.
- Why it matters: This tells us that validation, launch, and user delivery are intentionally presented as one chain.
- Modern transfer: Keep "track test -> launch -> delivery" as a signature sequence in the skill.

### 2025 — Product line broadens
- Observation: The official domestic site shows 500RR, 500F, and 820RR as key product pillars.
- Why it matters: The repo should not compress Zhang Xue into a single halo bike. He is operating a platform ambition, not a one-model story.
- Modern transfer: Product judgment in the skill should include platform logic, not only flagship logic.

### 2025 November — WorldSSP entry announcement crystallizes external proof path
- Observation: WorldSBK officially announced that ZXMOTO would join the 2026 WorldSSP field with Evan Bros Racing.
- Why it matters: This is a high-trust external confirmation that the international competition route was no longer rumor or aspiration.
- Modern transfer: The skill should treat official championship entry as a hard boundary between ambition and verified participation.

### 2026 February — Early competitive signs in WorldSSP
- Observation: ZXMOTO's global site and WorldSBK reporting both note an early front-row milestone at Phillip Island.
- Why it matters: Before winning, the team was already generating external evidence that the project was technically credible.
- Modern transfer: This matters because the repo should value stepwise proof, not only headline victories.

### 2026 March 29 — Historic Portimao double win
- Observation: Official WorldSBK reporting records two WorldSSP wins for ZXMOTO at Portimao and the official results PDF lists Debise as winner in both RC1 and RC2 for ZXM.
- Why it matters: This is the clearest external validation event in the public record so far.
- Modern transfer: Use this as proof that race validation is central to the skill, while refusing to let one weekend stand in for every other layer of truth.

### 2026 March 31 and after — Explosion of public attention
- Observation: Media coverage after Portimao rapidly reframed Zhang Xue as a symbol of Chinese manufacturing confidence, founder grit, and industrial self-belief.
- Why it matters: This is where the corpus becomes riskier. Attention rises, but so do mythology, overreach, and emotional distortion.

### 2026 April 18 — Snapshot boundary
- Observation: This skill is frozen to a specific public-material date rather than pretending to update in real time.
- Why it matters: Zhang Xue's public visibility is rising quickly, which means source quality and myth inflation are both moving targets.
- Modern transfer: Everything after this date should be treated as future revision work, not silently assumed.

## Role boundaries

### Rider and mechanic before founder symbol
- Observation: The strongest early source pattern shows Zhang Xue as someone formed in technical and riding contexts before he became a brand figure.
- Why it matters: This prevents the repo from being built backward from fame.
- Modern transfer: When the skill answers a problem, it should sound like an operator who knows what breaks, not a pure motivational persona.

### Product builder, not merely race promoter
- Observation: Official company materials foreground product history, engineering, and team background alongside racing.
- Why it matters: This suggests that competition is meant to legitimize product and engineering claims, not replace them.
- Modern transfer: Answers should always bring validation back to the product system.

### Founder judgment under hard constraints
- Observation: The post-KOVE phase sharpens the profile into someone making concentrated bets under resource and experience limits.
- Why it matters: This is one of the best reasons to distill him as a skill: priority judgment under pressure.
- Modern transfer: The repo should emphasize bottlenecks, sequence, and leverage.

### Industrial confidence with engineering obligations
- Observation: Statements like "if there is a blueprint, China can build it" are not just patriotic lines; in context they are claims about industrial capability and execution.
- Why it matters: Detached from context, such lines become hype. In context, they speak to supply chain depth, machining ability, and engineering confidence.
- Modern transfer: The skill must preserve both ambition and obligation.

### Brand heroism is not enough
- Observation: The public story invites hero framing very easily.
- Why it matters: That is precisely why the repo has to keep product proof, manufacturing truth, and organization depth in the foreground.
- Modern transfer: Never let the repo drift into pure founder mythology.

## Source classes

### Official ZXMOTO corporate biography
- Observation: The official "about us" material is a high-value source for chronology, role sequence, and brand self-definition.
- Why it matters: It anchors dates and role transitions with greater stability than short news summaries.
- Modern transfer: Use it for biography and value statements, but not as sole proof of performance claims.

### Official product pages
- Observation: Product pages are strong for specs, model lineup, and how the brand frames technical claims.
- Why it matters: They show what the company wants the world to believe about product identity.
- Modern transfer: Use them for declared capability, then compare against race proof or external validation.

### Official global news pages
- Observation: The global ZXMOTO site documents track tests, product launches, and race milestones in sequence.
- Why it matters: This helps reconstruct the brand's own validation narrative.
- Modern transfer: Use these pages to see how engineering, launch, and racing are tied together.

### WorldSBK / WorldSSP official reporting
- Observation: WorldSBK is among the highest-trust sources available for race participation, team entry, and result-level validation.
- Why it matters: It provides an external standard that the brand itself does not control.
- Modern transfer: Use it as the main proof layer for racing claims.

### Official result PDFs
- Observation: PDFs from the championship resource archive are the most precise source for results and standings.
- Why it matters: They protect the repo from retelling race outcomes loosely.
- Modern transfer: Use them when wording in news coverage becomes overly dramatic.

### Major media interviews
- Observation: Long interviews with mainstream outlets give access to tone, priority, and founder logic that specs alone cannot provide.
- Why it matters: They are essential for extracting expression DNA and decision framing.
- Modern transfer: Weight long interviews above short clip-based virality.

### Feature profiles after the Portimao wins
- Observation: Post-win profiles are useful but also highly unstable because attention spikes and narratives harden quickly.
- Why it matters: They often contain both valuable anecdotes and myth inflation.
- Modern transfer: Treat them as supporting material, not as the base layer.

### Encyclopedia-style summaries
- Observation: Public summary pages are useful for rough scaffolding but weak for fine-grained judgment logic.
- Why it matters: They compress controversy, ambiguity, and lineage.
- Modern transfer: Good for orientation, not for the skill's core logic.

### Short videos, clips, and fan edits
- Observation: This source class is abundant and dangerous.
- Why it matters: It is where "燃", "逆袭", and exaggerated founder hero narratives spread fastest.
- Modern transfer: Use them only to map hype patterns, not to define the skill.

## What belongs in the skill

### Engineering as the first lens
- Observation: The skill should begin from engineering because that is where Zhang Xue's public legitimacy is strongest.
- Why it matters: Without this, the repo collapses into founder charisma or motorcycle culture.
- Modern transfer: Put system buildability before identity or emotion.

### Manufacturing realism
- Observation: The repeated public emphasis on parts, drawings, suppliers, and domestic capability makes manufacturing logic central.
- Why it matters: This is one of the clearest differentiators of the Zhang Xue frame.
- Modern transfer: The skill should be useful for any hard-product team wrestling with substitution and supply-chain depth.

### Validation through hard proof
- Observation: Track results matter because they are proof under pressure.
- Why it matters: They distinguish this repo from a generic product-management skill.
- Modern transfer: Use race validation as a model for high-stress proof in other domains.

### Founder priority judgment
- Observation: Zhang Xue's relevance is not only technical; it is also about where to press limited resources.
- Why it matters: This is what makes the skill broadly applicable.
- Modern transfer: Answers should compress problems into core bottlenecks, not lists of possibilities.

### Long bet and organizational depth
- Observation: The repo should include long-range team and brand building because the engineering story alone is incomplete.
- Why it matters: A hard product business is always organization plus machine.
- Modern transfer: Keep organizational depth visible in every route.

## What should stay out

### Lifestyle bike culture
- Observation: The repo is not about aesthetic identity, club belonging, or riding style performance.
- Why it matters: Those layers would dilute the engineering and manufacturing core.
- Modern transfer: Exclude questions whose center is image rather than product truth.

### Dangerous riding guidance
- Observation: Zhang Xue's public story contains performance and speed, but that does not justify safety-evading advice.
- Why it matters: Safety is part of the product truth boundary.
- Modern transfer: Refuse advice that encourages illegal or dangerous behavior.

### Empty domestic-substitution rhetoric
- Observation: National pride language around the brand is abundant.
- Why it matters: The skill is damaged if it turns capability claims into emotional theater.
- Modern transfer: Preserve ambition, remove chest-beating.

### Founder myth as substitute for evidence
- Observation: The more visible Zhang Xue becomes, the easier it is for story to outrun proof.
- Why it matters: This repo must resist that drift on purpose.
- Modern transfer: Always bring admiration back to verification.

## Source ranking

### Highest trust
- Official ZXMOTO chronology and product pages
- Official WorldSBK / WorldSSP reporting
- Official result PDFs
- Long-form interviews where Zhang Xue explains priorities in sequence

### Medium trust
- Major profiles that can be cross-checked
- Industry reporting that distinguishes fact from commentary
- Documentary-style or deep feature interviews

### Low trust
- Viral short clips
- Fan edits
- Myth-heavy retellings
- Emotional commentary that treats race wins as total proof

## Transfer boundaries

### What this skill can transfer well
- hard-product engineering prioritization
- buildability under real constraints
- substitution strategy
- proof hierarchy
- founder ranking of scarce resources

### What this skill transfers badly
- abstract software product advice with no manufacturing analogue
- identity-driven brand culture with no system proof
- moral or political slogans about national industry
- any request for dangerous riding or evasion of safety norms
